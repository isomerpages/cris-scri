<h4><strong>Dr Mungun-Ulzii Khurelbaatar </strong><br></h4>
<p>Director, National Cardiovascular Center, Third State Central Hospital
<br>President, Mongolian Society of Cardiologists
<br>Arrhythmia Consultant, Second State Central Hospital</p>
<p>Dr. Mungun-Ulzii Khurelbaatar is a leading figure in Mongolian cardiology.
After graduating from medical school in 2004, he pursued a Master of Science
and cardiology training at National Yang Ming University and Taipei Hospital
in Taiwan. Since 2007, he has been serving at the Third State Central Hospital
as a cardiologist, where he advanced his expertise in interventional cardiology
and arrhythmia management through training in Germany and Taiwan.</p>
<p>A graduate of the Diploma of Advanced Studies in Cardiac Arrhythmia Management
(DAS-CAM), Dr. Khurelbaatar pioneered Mongolia’s first cardiac implantable
electronic device (CIED) therapy programs, including pacemaker, ICD, CRT,
and CCM implantation. He also spearheaded the establishment of Mongolia’s
first Electrophysiology (EP) laboratory, marking a milestone in national
cardiac care.</p>
<p>Committed to capacity building, he trained the first generation of Mongolian
arrhythmia specialists and founded the Cardiac Rhythmology Center. As Director
of the National Cardiovascular Center and President of the Mongolian Society
of Cardiologists, Dr. Khurelbaatar continues to lead transformative developments
in cardiovascular care and education.</p>
